Hospital coordination response: **When to Send a Patient with Severe Injury to the Hospital:**

Yes, any patient with a severe injury should be transported to the hospital immediately. Severe injuries can include:

* Open wounds or fractures
* Significant bleeding
* Head injuries
* Abdominal pain
* Chest pain
* Shortness of breath

**Transport Methods:**

The most appropriate transport method depends on the patient's condition and the available resources. Options include:

* **Ambulance (Ground Transport):** This is the most common method, suitable for most severe injuries that are stable enough to be transported by road.
* **Helicopter (Air Transport):** This is preferred for patients with life-threatening injuries or those who need specialized care not available at nearby hospitals.
* **Private Vehicle:** If an ambulance or helicopter is not available, a private vehicle may be used as a last resort. However, it's crucial to ensure the patient is properly supported and protected.

**Additional Considerations:**

* **Patient Stability:** Prioritize transporting the most stable patients first unless their injuries are life-threatening.
* **Triage:** If multiple patients are injured, use triage principles to determine the order of transport based on the severity of their injuries.
* **Communication:** Inform the receiving hospital of the patient's injuries and any initial treatments provided.
* **Follow-up:** Ensure the patient is transferred to the appropriate medical facility and receives the necessary care.

Administering Singulair for Asthma.
Warning: Side effects - hives , blisters, severe itching; difficult breathing; swelling of your face, lips, tongue, or throat. Tell your doctor right away if you have signs of blood vessel inflammation: flu-like symptoms, severe sinus pain, a skin rash, numbness or a "pins and needles" feeling in your arms or legs. Some people using Singulair have had new or worsening mental problems. Stop taking this medicine and call your doctor right away if you have unusual changes in mood or behavior, such as: agitation, aggression, feeling restless or irritable; anxiety , depression, confusion, problems with memory or attention; stuttering, tremors, uncontrolled muscle movements; suicidal thoughts or actions; hallucinations, sleep problems, vivid, dreams, sleep-walking; or compulsive or repetitive behaviors. Common Singulair side effects may include: stomach pain, diarrhea ; fever or other flu symptoms; ear pain or full feeling, trouble hearing; headache ; or cold symptoms such as runny or stuffy nose, sinus pain, cough, sore throat .
